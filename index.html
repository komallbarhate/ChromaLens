How the Color Mapping Works

01

RGB → HSL Conversion

Every pixel's RGB values are converted to Hue, Saturation, and Lightness — a perceptually uniform colour space that mirrors human vision.

02

Lightness Gating

Pixels with very high lightness (>88 %) are classified as White; very low lightness (<12 %) as Black; mid-lightness + low saturation as Gray.

03

Hue-Based Classification

Remaining pixels are sorted by hue angle into Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple, Pink, and Brown — matching the 11 universal categories.

04

Brown / Pink Refinement

Brown (low-saturation, warm hues) and Pink (high-lightness reds) are separated from Red and Orange using combined saturation-lightness thresholds.
